Question 7.

Define direction. Explain the principles of direction. (8-Marks) (3b)

(June/July 2018)

7.1 Definition & Meaning:

Business organizations have always given due importance to its managers who are capable of leading others. A manager needs to use various ways to lead, motivate and inspire the subordinates and to communicate with them suitably.

According to Koonz and O'Donnel, "Direction is a complex function that includes all those activities which are designed to encourage subordinates to work effectively and efficiently in both the short and long term".

i. Direction is an important managerial function which stimulates the organization and its staff to execute the plans. Manager should give direction to his subordinates through orders and supervise the work to ensure plans and policies. It breaks down into 2 activities.

ii. Giving orders to employees.

iii. Leading and motivating them.

7.2 Principles of Direction

7.2.1 Creation of synergy of individual efforts . We know that Organizations comprise of numerous individuals with diverse set of personal objectives, beliefs and values. An effective direction would align their personal objectives towards the organizational objectives so that individuals start behaving like a coherent team, thus resulting in synergy of individual efforts.

7.2.2 Unity of command : People tend to get confused when they get direction and orders from more than one superior. Some of these orders may be completely divergent to each other, thus adding to the misery of the subordinates who are supposed to follow them. Therefore, unity of command and direction is a useful principle.

7.2.3 Close interaction with subordinates: It is important for the bosses to have close interaction with their subordinates to guide them, mentor them and empathize with them. The human contact creates a unique sense of direction, which at times is difficult to achieve by other modes of communication in this networked world.

7.2.4 Feedback and suggestions for improvement: Occasional feedbacks on performance, particularly the positive feedbacks from superiors to subordinates are encouraging for them. Negative feedbacks should be inter-ensconced with constructive suggestions for improvement so as to minimize the hurt-feeling on part of the subordinates.

7.2.5 Motivate, enthuse and enthrall: Motivation lies at the heart of direction. People should be directed in such a way that they feel motivated, enthused and fascinated to follow the directions given to them whole-heartedly.

7.2.6 Lead by example: It is the principle which says "preach what you practice". Thus, followers understand the directions of their leader much better, when the leader follows herself what she preaches. For example, a boss who is very strict about the timely arrival of employees to office, need to be punctual himself.


Question 8.

Define leadership. Explain the various leaderships styles. (8-Marks) (4a)

(June/July 2018)

8.1 DEFINITIONS AND MEANING OF LEADERSHIP

There are many definitions of leadership. Some of the definitions of leadership are reproduced below:

i. "Leadership" according to Alford and Beatty" is the ability to secure desirable actions from a group of followers voluntarily, without the use of coercion".

ii. According to Chester I. Barnard, "It (leadership) refers to the quality of the behaviour of the individual whereby they guide people on their activities in organized efforts".

iii. According to Terry, "A leader shows the way by his own example. He is not a pusher; he pulls rather than pushes".

iv. According to Koontz and O' Donnell - Managerial leadership is "The ability to exert interpersonal influence by means of communication, towards the achievement of a goal". Since managers get things done through people, their success depends, to a considerable extent upon their ability to provide leadership.

8.2 LEADERSHIP STYLES

There are three important approaches of leadership, they are

(a) Traits approach

(b) Behavioral approach and

(c) Contingency approach

8.2.1 Traits approach:

i. Leader should have good health and physical fitness, intelligence, mental and physical energy, knowledge of human relation, personal motivation, communication skills, qualities of a leader.

Qualities can be grouped into three categories:

a. Physical qualities

b. Moral qualities

c. Mental qualities

Physical qualities: Leader must have physical energy to stand up to stress and strain of his office and responsibility.

Moral qualities: This quality helps to stick on his goal and to own the responsibility of his action.

Mental qualities: A leader must possess this quality to be able to motivate his subordinates into action.

ii. Qualities of head and heart like

a. Emotional and social maturity

b. Knowledge

c. Ability to see the things and understand them for another point of view

d. Ability to react to the behavior of others in cool. But it should be objective

e. Initiative

8.2.2 Behavior approach:

i. Motivation: Positive motivation makes his subordinates to rewards, negative motivation makes his subordinates punishment.

ii. Authority: Leadership styles can be automatic, democratic or free region.

a. Autocratic: Autocratic leader take decision without consulting subordinates. They simply pass the orders and it should be obeyed by subordinates. This enforces discipline and obedience. Decision making is completely centralized. Communication is one way always in the form of instructions and orders. Subordinates should depend on leader for everything. Leader just supervise his subordinates.

b. Democratic on consultive leadership: Leaders consult their subordinates before taking decision. It provides complete freedom to group members and result in two-way communication emphasis is given co-operation and protecting group interact. Group members get an opportunity (o express their opinion. Productivity is increased,

c. Free rein leadership: Free rein leaders leaves the subordinates with complete freedom. Subordinates can set their plans and develop plans. Decision are made by subordinates. Leaders do not involve in it. Leaders will advise. Leader will not control followers. Here subordinates must be knowledgeable, well trained and self-motivated it develops managerial skills and creativity.

8.2.3 Contingency approach:

i. Fielders model

ii. Hersy and Blanchard's model

iii. Robert j Houser's model

I. Fielders model: Leadership style depends on situation favour. It depends on three factors.

a. Leader member relation - leader member relationship is good only if leader is respected.

b. Task structure - Task oriented leader is most effective in extreme situation where situation is favourable or unfavourable,

c. Position power- Position power leadership means leader is highly positioned and highly powered. The role of the leadership is effective.

II. Hersey and Blanchard model: -

a. This model contains four stages, initial phase, when subordinates first enter into organization they need to be instructed and rules and regulations are expressed. Manager should be low in relationship because subordinates cannot be treated as colleagues.

b. As subordinates matures, manager trust increases grow high relationship between manager and subordinates. Subordinate is needed to be in high task.

c. In third phase subordinates is so matured no need of manager but still required for support finally subordinates become highly matured, motivated and directing the manager.

III. Robert j houses model

There can be 4 types of leaderships :-

a. Directive - Directive leader is one who gives what to do and howto do.

b. Supportive - Supportive leader gives respect to subordinates and will be encouraging with positive exchange.

c. Achievement oriented - Achievement oriented leader is one who sets goals, well confident about subordinates and completes the task.

d. Participative - Participative leader is one who takes decisions after consulting his subordinates.
